# SeQwens Guide - Easy Answers

## Phase 1 — Getting Started With SeQwens

### 1. Introduction to SeQwens
SeQwens is a simple online tool for tax experts and accounting firms. It helps manage clients, handle documents, automate work, and let teams work together. You use it through a web dashboard and a special client area.

### 2. Logging Into SeQwens for the First Time
Go to the SeQwens login page. Put in your email and password. If it's your first time, you might need to set up extra security like a code sent to your phone. After logging in, if you have different roles or firms, the system will help you choose.

### 3. Navigating the SeQwens Dashboard
The main screen has a top bar with menus and maybe a switcher for roles/firms. The middle shows your main tasks. What you see depends on your role: bosses see client lists, workers see their jobs, clients see their own stuff.

### 4. Understanding User Roles in SeQwens
- **Super Admin**: Controls the whole system
- **Firm Admin**: Manages the company, team, and clients
- **Tax Preparer/Staff**: Does client work and tasks
- **Client/Taxpayer**: Uses the portal to share documents

### 5. Setting Up Your Firm Profile
If you're a Firm Admin, go to Settings > Firm Profile. Add your company name, address, logo, phone, and tax info. This info shows up in emails to clients.

### 6. Managing Account Settings
Click your name in the top right > Settings. Change your info, password, what notifications you get, and security like two-step login.

## Phase 2 — Team Setup

### 7. Inviting Team Members to Your Firm
As Firm Admin, go to Team > Invite Member. Type their email and pick their job role. Send the invite – they get an email with a link to join.

### 8. Assigning Roles to Team Members
When you invite someone or edit their profile, choose their role. Roles are like Firm Admin (can do everything) or Tax Preparer (works on assigned clients). Use the switcher at the top to change views.

### 9. Managing Team Member Profiles
In the Team section, see all members. Edit their roles, update contact info, or turn off their accounts. Check logs to see what they've done.

### 10. Assigning Clients to Team Members
In a client's profile, pick "Assign Team Member" from the menu. Choose who should handle that client. You can pick more than one person.

### 11. Understanding Automatic Client Assignment
Go to Settings > Client Assignment. Set rules like "give new clients to the person with least work" or "assign based on client type." The system does this automatically.

## Phase 3 — Client Management

### 12. Creating a Client Profile Manually
Go to Clients > Add Client. Fill in name, email, phone, and ID number. Add more details like address or notes if you want. Save it.

### 13. Sending a Client Portal Invitation
In the client's profile, click "Send Portal Invitation." It emails them a safe link to join their own area in SeQwens.

### 14. What the Client Invitation Email Looks Like
The email has your firm's logo and info. It gives a secure link, tells them how to upload files, and has contact details for help.

### 15. Client Portal Walkthrough
Clients log in and see their main screen: upload area, messages, task updates, and settings. It's easy to use for sharing documents.

### 16. Uploading Documents as a Client
In the portal, click "Upload Documents" or drag files there. Pick what type of document it is and add notes. It's safe and private.

### 17. Reviewing Uploaded Client Documents
Your team sees new files in their dashboard. They can check, download, add notes, or ask for more info. Then mark as done.

### 18. Bulk Importing Clients
Go to Clients > Import. Download the sample file, fill it with client info (name, email, etc.), then upload it. The system adds them all at once.

### 19. Understanding Bulk Import CSV Template
The sample file has columns for: First Name, Last Name, Email, Phone, Address, ID Number, Tax Year, Labels. Some columns are required, others optional.

### 20. Fixing Common Bulk Import Errors
Common problems: wrong email format, missing needed info, or duplicate emails. Look at the error list, fix the file, and try again. Make sure numbers are right.

## Phase 4 — Client Organization

### 21. Using Tags to Organize Clients
In client profiles or when adding many, add labels like "Important" or "Business." Tags help you find and group clients easily.

### 22. Editing and Removing Client Tags
In the client's details, click on a tag to change or delete it. Changes happen right away everywhere.

### 23. Searching and Filtering Clients
Use the search box for names or emails. Add filters by tags, who they're assigned to, status, or dates. Save your favorite filters.

### 24. Viewing Client Activity History
In the client's profile, go to Activity tab. See all actions: uploads, messages, tasks, with dates and times.

### 25. Managing Archived Clients
Go to Clients > Archived. See old inactive clients. Bring them back or delete forever. They don't show in active lists but keep their history.

## Phase 5 — Document Management

### 26. Requesting Documents From Clients
In the client's profile or tasks, make a request. Pick document types, set deadlines, add instructions. Clients get notified.

### 27. How Clients Receive Document Requests
Clients get an email about the request. In their portal, they see it in a special "Requested" area with clear steps and dates.

### 28. Uploading Documents Internally
Team members can upload files directly to a client's profile in the Documents tab. Pick the file, type, and notes.

### 29. Organizing Client Documents
Use folders and tags in Documents. Make custom folders, add type tags, and search to find files fast.

### 30. Tracking Missing Documents
Look at the Missing Documents report. It shows what's still needed by client and due date. Send reminders automatically or by hand.

## Phase 6 — Tasks and Work Management

### 31. Creating Tasks in SeQwens
Go to Tasks > New Task. Pick who does it, give it a title, description, priority, and due date. Link it to a client if needed.

### 32. Assigning Tasks to Team Members
When making or editing a task, choose from the team list. It shows up in their dashboard with alerts. Change it anytime.

### 33. Setting Task Due Dates and Priorities
Pick dates from the calendar. Choose how important it is (Low to Urgent). The system reminds you as time runs out.

### 34. Tracking Task Progress
Tasks show status like "To Do" or "Done." Update with notes. See them in widgets or full views.

### 35. Completing and Closing Tasks
Mark as done with notes. Closed tasks go to archive but stay searchable. Make reports if needed.

## Phase 7 — Workflow Automation

### 36. Introduction to SeQwens Workflows
Workflows make repetitive jobs automatic, like tax seasons. Set up steps that happen on their own when something starts.

### 37. Creating a Workflow Template
In Workflows, make a new one. Set what triggers it (like a new client), then add steps with who does what and when.

### 38. Assigning Workflows to Clients
Apply manually in client profiles or set it to start automatically. It begins when conditions match.

### 39. Automatic Task Creation With Workflows
When triggered, it makes tasks for team members one by one. Each done task starts the next until finished.

### 40. Using Tags to Trigger Workflows
Set workflows to start when you add certain tags to clients. Like "Tax Time 2024" could begin the whole process.

## Phase 8 — Communication Tools

### 41. Sending Messages to Clients
In the client's profile, go to Messages. Write secure messages that they see in their portal. Add files and see if read.

### 42. Viewing Client Conversations
In the profile, see all messages in order with times and read status.

### 43. Notifications and Alerts
In Settings, pick what alerts you get: emails, texts for new files, assignments, deadlines, or activity. See them live in the dashboard.

### 44. Email Communication Integration
Messages use your firm's look. Clients reply and it makes a thread. Works with your email for easy talk.

## Phase 9 — E-Signature & Compliance

### 45. Sending Documents for E-Signature
Upload the document and use E-Signature. Mark where to sign, add notes, and send. Uses HelloSign for safety.

### 46. Signing Documents as a Client
Clients get an email with a link. In the portal, they review and sign electronically – draw, type, or upload their signature.

### 47. Tracking Signature Status
In document details, see if it's Sent, Viewed, Signed, or Declined. Get alerts when done or needing action.

## Phase 10 — Monitoring Your Practice

### 48. Viewing Client Activity Logs
See detailed records of all client actions, changes, and events with who did what and when.

### 49. Monitoring Team Workload
Use dashboard charts showing assignments, completion, and balance. Adjust to keep everyone busy but not overwhelmed.

### 50. Best Practices for Managing Your Firm in SeQwens
Check logs often, use labels consistently, automate with workflows, train your team, watch client feedback, and keep up with updates.
